Zodiac Compatibility Checker

Discover the classical relationship between two zodiac animals using the Six Harmonies, Three Harmonies, Six Clashes, and Harms

The Six Harmonies (六合)

The Six Harmonies represent the six most naturally compatible zodiac pairings. These are animals whose Earthly Branches combine to produce a harmonious elemental transformation:

PairCombined ElementRelationship Quality
Rat & OxEarthStability, loyalty, deep mutual support
Tiger & PigWoodGenerosity, warmth, shared idealism
Rabbit & DogFireDevotion, trust, emotional security
Dragon & RoosterMetalAmbition, precision, mutual admiration
Snake & MonkeyWaterIntelligence, adaptability, strategic alliance
Horse & GoatFireCreativity, passion, artistic synergy

The Three Harmonies (三合)

The Three Harmonies group animals that share an elemental affinity through their position on the zodiac wheel, forming cooperative triads:

  • Water Triad: Monkey, Rat, Dragon — Shēn-Zǐ-Chén (申子辰)
  • Wood Triad: Tiger, Horse, Dog — Yín-Wǔ-Xū (寅午戌)
  • Fire Triad: Snake, Rooster, Ox — Sì-Yǒu-Chǒu (巳酉丑)
  • Metal Triad: Pig, Rabbit, Goat — Hài-Mǎo-Wèi (亥卯未)

Animals within the same triad share cooperative energy and tend to understand each other's motivations intuitively.

The Six Clashes (六衝)

The Six Clashes pair animals that sit directly opposite each other on the zodiac wheel. Their energies are fundamentally opposed:

  • Rat ↔ Horse — Water vs Fire; careful planning vs impulsive freedom
  • Ox ↔ Goat — Earthy pragmatism vs artistic sensitivity
  • Tiger ↔ Monkey — Bold directness vs clever manoeuvring
  • Rabbit ↔ Rooster — Diplomatic gentleness vs frank outspokenness
  • Dragon ↔ Dog — Grandiose ambition vs grounded loyalty
  • Snake ↔ Pig — Calculated reserve vs open generosity

Clashing pairs can produce tension, but classical analysis notes that awareness of these dynamics allows individuals to transform conflict into complementary growth. A clash does not doom a relationship — it signals where conscious effort is needed.

The Six Harms (六害)

The Six Harms describe pairings where hidden resentments and misunderstandings tend to develop over time:

  • Rat & Goat
  • Ox & Horse
  • Tiger & Snake
  • Rabbit & Dragon
  • Monkey & Pig
  • Rooster & Dog

Harms are subtler than clashes. While clashes are overt conflicts, harms manifest as gradual erosion of trust or unexplained friction that builds over time.

A Classical Perspective

Zodiac compatibility based solely on the birth year is the most general level of analysis. A complete assessment in classical Chinese astrology would examine the full BaZi chart (all four pillars), the five element balance of both individuals, and the interactions between all eight Earthly Branches and Heavenly Stems in their combined charts.

This tool offers a starting point grounded in classical relationships between the twelve Earthly Branches. For deeper analysis, consult a qualified BaZi practitioner.
